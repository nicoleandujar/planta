My offering to the medicine community

By Nicole KetzalYolotMazatl Andujar 13 May 2021 • Photo by: KetzalYolotMazatl

P L A N T A was birhted as a love project. The intention with this website is to help guide people coming into the world of ancestral plant medicine and find safe information about the medicines, the ceremonies, what to expect, how to prepare, suggested protocols, news about the medicines worldwide, and much more.

The site is filled with curated articles from writers all over the world, who share their wisdom, knowledge and expertise in order to continue evolving in the huge task of healing from the inside that brave individuals take upon themselves to do this inner work with plant medicine.

My name is Nicole KetzalYoloMazatl and I have been part of the medicine comunity in South Florida for over 12 years. I am a Moondancer in Teotihuacan, Mexico, a Vision Quester, ceremomy singer, reiki practitioner, yoga teacher, graphic designer, and part of the Inti Runa Native American Church here in Florida. I have the privilege of having met great teachers during the past dedade who continue to share the wisdom of the many traditions from where the various medicines come from. During the past 4 years, I have been working on a Master's degree in Interactive Media from the University of Miami. This work has taken me away from the ceremony life I love and has become such a big part of my life. This project is the culmination of these past few years that I undertook as a student once again.

I wanted to create a safe space for people to be able to learn and expand their knowledge aboout plant medicine, for people to know what questions to ask, how to prepare and where these medicines come from, how they are being used and cared for by the same people who were kind enough to share them outside of the jungle or the desert and the many parts of the world where they come form.

It is always truly a deep honor for me to be able to share this project. It has been many years in the making and I just want to be able to help people, help guide them through any questions they may have about attending a native ceremony.
